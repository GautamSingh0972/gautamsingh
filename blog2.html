Effect of the US-Iran War on the World Economy

Why geopolitical conflict matters for global markets.

Conflict in major regions of the world can quickly affect the global economy. When countries such as the United States and Iran are involved, the impact can spread through energy markets, trade routes, and investor confidence.

One of the most important channels is oil. Any major disruption in energy supplies can push prices higher, affecting transportation costs, inflation, and everyday living costs around the world.

These events also influence financial markets because investors often react strongly to uncertainty. For this reason, understanding geopolitics is essential for anyone interested in economics and finance.
